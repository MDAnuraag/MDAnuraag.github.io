Short notes on interpretation errors I have encountered in my own work.

These are not full case studies. Each note isolates one mistake: the tempting interpretation, why it fails, and what a better reading requires.

---

## Inference errors

- [Spectra are constraints, not fingerprints](/notes/spectra-constraints/) — when agreement does not mean identification
- [Good fits don't imply identifiability](/notes/good-fits/) — when a low residual hides multiple solutions
- [Sensitivity is not identifiability](/notes/sensitivity/) — when a signal responds to a parameter but cannot isolate it
- [Averaging destroys information irreversibly](/notes/averaging/) — when a mean is mistaken for a distribution
- [Composition is not structure](/notes/composition-not-structure/) — when a formula hides configurational freedom
- [Simulation is not deployment evidence](/notes/simulation-not-deployment/) — when controlled improvement is overextended
